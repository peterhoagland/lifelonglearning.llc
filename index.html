Who We Serve

Our goal is to provide our services to ALL children and adults who are struggling academically, professionally, relationally, and emotionally:

  • any child or adult with known learning challenges: dyslexia, ADHD, anxiety, Autism, Down syndrome, Fetal Alcohol Syndrome, Reactive Attachment Disorder, Post Concussion Syndrome, Post Traumatic Stress Disorder, language and sensory processing disorders, neurodevelopmental and intellectual disabilities, dysgraphia, dyscalculia, and memory struggles
  • post-adoptive children and foster care children who are experiencing challenges
  • the first-grader trying so hard to learn to read but getting more and more frustrated
  • the fourth-grader spending hours on homework, but unable to recall the content for the test
  • the young athlete unable to remember the coach's plays and may have Post Concussion Syndrome
  • the honors student in middle school who lacks basic study skills
  • the high school student unable to keep up struggles to remember what they read, and can't listen and take notes
  • the college student or who is struggling with a huge workload
  • Business Professionals, Adult Learners, and Aging Adults who are in a stressful work environment or returning to school. Also, adults who are concerned that their memory and skills "just aren't what they used to be" or have a family history of dementia or Alzheimers.

Program Components

The completion of the program involves diligent fulfillment of the following:

Cognitive therapy Exercises: Equipping Minds Cognitive Development - 45-60 minutes a day

Neuro- Development Intervention: Primitive Reflex Movement therapy - 10 - 15 minutes a day

Sound Therapy: Mysoundtherapy.com and equippingminds.com. Designed to work synergistically with other treatments. Can be used several hours a day.

Time commitment: Students are mentored for 24 hours, two days a week over a 12 week period. Involved adult/guardian is present during the training sessions. Three days a week, guardians/parents mentor their child using the purchased program materials.
